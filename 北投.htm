Beitou Hot Spring Museum 

The Beitou Hot Spring Museum was completed in 1913 during the Japanese rule in Taiwan, covering an area of ​​about 700 square meters . The British-style two-storey brick building has been listed as a third-class historic site in Taiwan. The exhibition areas on the first and second floors are themed on the development history of Beitou Hot Springs, including the development of hot spring township, the history of Ketagalan. And, you don't need to buy ticket to enter.


Beitou Hot Spring Museum is open from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm. In order to maintain the quality of visits, there is a maximum number of people in the museum, and people need to change into slippers that provided by the museum.

Beitou Museum

The museum was built in the 1920s with exquisite workmanship. It is one of the largest single Japanese-style pure wooden buildings in Taiwan. During the Japanese occupation period, it was a club for Japanese officers. It is now privately owned, but you can go in and visit it, but you need to pay. It doesn't take a lot of time to come here, it's about 20 minutes by car from downtown Taipei.

The museum can be divided into three parts: one is the cultural relics museum, the other is the Zen Garden, which provides a good place for drinking tea and studying Chinese tea art, and the third is the plow house.

Geothermal Valley

Geothermal Valley is located next to Beitou Park in Beitou District, Taipei City. It is one of the sources of Beitou hot springs, with a total area of ​​about 3,500 square meters. It is the hot spring with the highest water temperature in the Datun Mountain Group.

In the early years, the geothermal valley was a place for tourists to cook and play. Not only were scalding accidents prone to occur, but also the quality of the hot spring water was polluted, causing the guardrails to often collapse. Therefore, the geothermal valley was refurbished in 1994 by the water supply department, and railings were installed around the hot spring pool. The gazebo and the water-friendly ditch, although you can't cook there, you can soak your feet there, so that everyone can still enjoy the hot spring when they come to the geothermal valley.

Former Japanese Garrison Hospital

The former Japanese military garrison hospital is a Japanese-style building transplanted from Japan during the Japanese rule in Taiwan . It is also the predecessor of the Beitou Branch of the General Hospital of the Three Armed Forces. It is designated as a historic site in Taipei City. The antique building is built of wood. There are also free hot spring here. The appearance of the pool and historic buildings is well preserved, and the light green and white paint on the wooden buildings are very beautiful.

Teng Yun Temple

Teng Yun Temple, established in the year 1896, is Taiwan's first privately-owned hot spring resort.

In the year it was founded, its founder, Genzo Hirata, came to Beitou and unexpectedly discovered the therapeutic benefits of its hot springs. He decided to stay and recuperate here, eventually establishing a hot spring resort. He also engaged in other meaningful endeavors that made significant contributions to the local development of Beitou.
